afterthought

Sauce is failing to sleep, lying on his back, tail curled over his body. He stares upwards blankly at the ceiling, with a look of solemn. He's trapped in thought.

Sauce (in thought): i cant stop thinking about it
was what i did ok?
he looked uncomfortable
will he avoid me?
i dont know him yet
shit how do i explain
im so naive
not again...

His spiral is interrupted by Granola stopping by his room.

Granola: Sauce? you have your eyes on, are you ok?

Sauce doesn't feel ready to reply, only emitting a soft "mmh...". Granola leans into the room some more, growing worried.

Granola: bud, it pains me every time i see you like this
i can't relate with you, but i hate that you have to deal with this

After some thinking, Sauce sits up and begins sharing.

Sauce: i have no baseline for what to do—like what is and isnt ok
i cant tell that
my body wasnt made for that
Granola: well you've learned a good amount of social norms
i tihnk you're in an ok place

Sauce is quick to interrupt, however.

Sauce: he was uneasy around me
Granola: and?

Sauce pauses for a moment more before stating his true concern.

Sauce: i dont want him to think of me wrongly

Granola attempt to reassure Sauce.

Granola: that's inevitable
there's probably hundreds who underestimate you, but you still worry after meeting someone
it isn't worth fighting that hard for it
it isn't worth repeating... this... every time

Sauce objects harshly.

Sauce: its easy for you to say
you already know all morals, but i have no way to understand you
it doesnt help coming from you

Granola is stunned by the comment and turns away, understanding dawning on him. Sauce realizes the tone of his speech and panics, stammering to correct what he said.

Sauce: wait
no
um
uh
Granola: no no, i understand
Sauce: no you— please keep trying

Granola doesn't look back. Only following a moment to think, he has an epiphany.

Granola: well...
you don't truly know how he feels, right?
Sauce: yeah...?
Granola: i'm sure he's not that irked by you
if you come across him, just check in and show you care
don't flake out and leave him guessing!

Granola turns around to exit the room, checking over his shoulder to state one more thing.

Granola: get some sleep though, please...

Sauce looks down towards his tail, deliberating.

Sauce: ...alright

Granola exits. Sauce takes his eyes out, setting them on the desk nearby, then rolls onto his side.

I think a common assumption that people go to when they look at Sauce is that he's the common "squeezable cute thing" that a lot of similar characters share. This sentiment is entirely natural, and honestly it was the intent I had when I originally designed Sauce. However, I've grown to "understand" him as a character much more deeply, and he seems to hinge partly on the neurodivergency that I have, just in a different way than I would relate.

This was correctly spotted by someone prior to this comic: Sauce doesn't have a moral basis unlike other beings, so what is morally "correct" or "incorrect" from situation to situation doesn't come as intuitive to him, and he has to spend a great amount of effort attempting to deduce this from the people around him. He also tends to spend much of the time after the fact reflecting on any situations which are as described—where he isn't sure if what happened fit into his understanding of the social world.

This is directly in contrast to Granola, who is loaded with all of the cultural lore any place has (much like Chester being an omniglot), though he's generally more analytical.

The comic you read is meant to directly show this through the lens of the two. I think it's even better demonstrated in-comic than here, but it's up to your interpretation. I also just want to wash away the common understanding of who (or really what) Sauce is.

Mental stuff out the way, there's some extra minor details that I want to add here.

It may come as a little strange to have Granola intuit Sauce as awake by his eyes "being on". Sauce's eyes very obviously protrude outside of the head, but that's their natural resting position as the head itself doesn't actually have a slot or socket for them to embed into. When Sauce does close his eyes, they're literally sucked into the head, shoving away the material to fit the eyes, which causes it to exert a lot of pressure outwards. Because of this, it becomes exhausing to close them for a long period of time (ironic to how we use it), so literally detaching them from his head serves to cut the input without requiring constant effort.

I didn't properly express it in text-form, but Granola's shock at Sauce's backlash towards him somewhat... saddened him? Dissapointed him? I'm not sure the correct term for it, but it hurts to be told by a sibling that you're genuinely not helping. Of course, this isn't what Sauce intended (remember the earlier paragraphs). Granola isn't usually like this with anyone else; it's a familial behavior.
